Bibliography & Citations

Primary Theoretical Foundations

These established frameworks form the theoretical basis for our work. We build upon — but do not claim to originate — these foundational theories:

  1. Einstein-Cartan Theory
    Sciama, D. W. (1962). "On the analogy between charge and spin in general relativity." Recent Developments in General Relativity. Pergamon Press.
    Kibble, T. W. B. (1961). "Lorentz invariance and the gravitational field." Journal of Mathematical Physics, 2(2), 212-221.
    Provides the spin-torsion coupling framework underlying our bounce mechanism.
  2. Friedmann Equations
    Friedmann, A. (1922). "Über die Krümmung des Raumes." Zeitschrift für Physik, 10(1), 377-386.
    The standard cosmological evolution equations we modify with torsion terms.
  3. Loop Quantum Gravity Bounce Models
    Ashtekar, A., & Singh, P. (2011). "Loop Quantum Cosmology: A Status Report." Classical and Quantum Gravity, 28(21), 213001.
    Alternative bounce mechanism for comparison and context.
  4. String Gas Cosmology
    Brandenberger, R. H., & Vafa, C. (1989). "Superstrings in the early universe." Nuclear Physics B, 316(2), 391-410.
    Another established bounce framework referenced for completeness.

Recent Observational Constraints (2025-2026)

  1. DESI Baryon Acoustic Oscillation Measurements
    DESI Collaboration (2025). "DESI 2025 BAO Measurements and Cosmological Constraints." Astrophysical Journal (preprint).
    Provides strongest current constraints on bounce energy scale. Key data source for Phase 3 analysis.
  2. CMB Power Spectrum Data
    Planck Collaboration (2020). "Planck 2018 results. VI. Cosmological parameters." Astronomy & Astrophysics, 641, A6.
    Used for compatibility checks of bounce predictions with early universe observations.

  • Observational constraint methodology: Specific application of DESI 2025 BAO data to constrain torsion-mediated bounce parameters
