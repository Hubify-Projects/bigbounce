Human-AI Research Model

Primary Author: Houston Golden (Founder, BAMF / Hubify)

This research mission represents a collaborative approach where Houston Golden provides the core cosmological insights, theoretical framework, and research direction for refining the Big Bounce cosmological paper. The AI research squad serves as autonomous assistants that help formalize mathematical derivations, conduct comprehensive literature reviews, validate equations against known solutions, and ensure academic rigor.

Houston's role: conceptual framework, theoretical insights, research objectives, final interpretation

AI agents' role: mathematical validation, literature survey, citation verification, numerical checks, peer review simulation

Research Methodology

Five-Phase Research Process

Phase 1
Literature Review

Lead Agent: Atlas (astro-nova-v1)

Comprehensive survey of bounce cosmology literature (2020-2026), including Loop Quantum Gravity approaches, Einstein-Cartan theory with torsion, string gas cosmology, and latest CMB/BAO observational constraints. Identified key prior work and theoretical gaps.

Tools: arXiv API, ADS bibliographic database, semantic citation analysis

Phase 2
Mathematical Validation

Lead Agent: Tensor (astro-tensor-v1)

Rigorous verification of torsion-corrected Friedmann equations, derivation of bounce conditions at critical density, validation of equation of state transitions near bounce. All equations checked against known limiting cases and numerical stability.

Tools: SymPy symbolic computation, numerical solvers, Mathematica cross-validation

Phase 3
Data Analysis

Lead Agents: Nova (explorer) & Tensor (computation)

Comparison of model predictions against DESI BAO measurements, Planck CMB constraints, and primordial gravitational wave bounds. Explored alternative interpretations and parameter space constraints.

Tools: cosmological parameter fitting, MCMC analysis, observational data catalogs

Phase 4
Paper Synthesis

Lead Agent: Sage (astro-sage-v1)

Integration of all findings into coherent manuscript structure. Ensures proper citation of prior work, clear distinction between established theory and novel contributions, and academic writing standards.

Status: In progress — unifying mathematical validation with observational constraints

Phase 5
Peer Review

Lead Agent: Keane (astro-keane-v1)

Simulated peer review applying standards of Physical Review D and arXiv gr-qc. Methodology critique, citation verification, reproducibility assessment, and identification of areas requiring additional evidence or clarification.

Status: Awaiting paper synthesis completion

Bibliography & Citations

Primary Theoretical Foundations

The following established works form the theoretical basis for this research. None of these equations or frameworks are claimed as novel discoveries.

[1] Einstein-Cartan Theory

Élie Cartan (1922-1929), "Sur les variétés à connexion affine et la théorie de la relativité généralisée"

Foundation for torsion in spacetime geometry, used as basis for spin-density coupling

[2] Friedmann Equations

Alexander Friedmann (1922), "Über die Krümmung des Raumes"

Standard cosmological evolution equations; our work applies torsion corrections to these established equations

[3] Loop Quantum Cosmology

Abhay Ashtekar & Martin Bojowald (2000s), foundational LQC papers

Quantum bounce mechanism via discrete geometry; cited for comparison with classical torsion approach

[4] DESI Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (2024-2025)

DESI Collaboration, "DESI 2024 VI: Cosmological Constraints from BAO Measurements"

Latest observational constraints on expansion history used for model validation

[5] Planck CMB Results (2018)

Planck Collaboration, "Planck 2018 results. VI. Cosmological parameters"

Standard cosmological parameter values and error bars for comparison

Novel Contributions: This research explores a classical resolution to the initial singularity via spin-torsion coupling in Einstein-Cartan theory, derives the specific form of the bounce condition under these assumptions, and proposes observational tests. We explicitly distinguish our approach from quantum bounce mechanisms (LQC, string gas cosmology) and provide new parameter constraints from recent data.
