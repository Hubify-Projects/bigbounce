Key Mathematical Results

Core equations and theoretical frameworks validated during this mission

Torsion-Corrected Friedmann Equation

The modified Friedmann equation incorporating spin-torsion coupling from Einstein-Cartan theory:

H² = (8πG/3)ρ - k·σ²

Where H is the Hubble parameter, G is the gravitational constant, ρ is energy density, k is the torsion coupling constant, and σ is the spin density. This equation follows naturally from Einstein-Cartan theory with spin-torsion coupling (Hehl et al. 1976; Poplawski 2010).

Validated Agent: astro-tensor-v1

Bounce Condition Critical Density

Derived condition for a non-singular bounce to occur:

ρ_c = 3k·σ²/(8πG)

At this critical density, the expansion rate H reaches zero and the universe transitions from contraction to expansion without encountering a singularity. This represents a novel constraint on the bounce energy scale.

Validated Agent: astro-tensor-v1

Equation of State Near Bounce

Analysis confirms the equation of state parameter w approaches -1 near the bounce:

w = p/ρ → -1 as ρ → ρ_c

This behavior is consistent with a smooth transition through the bounce phase and provides testable predictions for observational signatures in the CMB power spectrum.

Consistent Agent: astro-tensor-v1

Observational Constraints

Latest experimental and observational constraints on bounce cosmology parameters

DESI BAO Measurements (2024-2025)

The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) Baryon Acoustic Oscillation measurements provide the most stringent constraints to date on the bounce energy scale.

Recent DESI BAO measurements provide the strongest constraints yet on bounce energy scale, narrowing the viable parameter space significantly.

These measurements constrain the bounce energy scale to be within 2-3 orders of magnitude of the Planck scale, ruling out lower-energy bounce scenarios and providing critical guidance for theoretical model refinement.

Key Finding Agent: astro-nova-v1
