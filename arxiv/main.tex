\begin{abstract}
Recent JWST observations reveal a striking 65--35\% galaxy spin asymmetry at high redshift, while the Hubble tension has reached $4.4\sigma$ and the $\sigma_8$ tension $2.5\sigma$---suggesting new physics beyond $\Lambda$CDM. We present a theoretical framework where dark energy emerges from quantum gravitational effects in a rotating universe born from a black hole interior.

Our model combines Loop Quantum Gravity with Einstein-Cartan torsion, deriving dark energy from a parity-violating quantum correction: $\Leff = \Lconst + c_\omega\omega^2$, where $\Lconst = (\alpha/M)\Dinf$. Given stringent isotropy bounds $(\omega/H)_0 < 5 \times 10^{-11}$, rotation is negligible for expansion but sources parity-odd observables. The model yields $H_0 = 69.2 \pm 0.8\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$ and $\sigma_8 = 0.785 \pm 0.016$ from modified early-universe physics, reducing tensions without fine-tuning ($10^5$ vs.\ $10^{120}$).

Key predictions: (1) galaxy spin dipole asymmetry following $A(z) = A_0(1+z)^{-p}e^{-qz}$ with hierarchical Bayesian fits yielding $A_0 \sim 0.003$; (2) ultra-low-$\ell$ CMB $E$-$B$ correlations detectable by LiteBIRD; (3) correlated cosmic anomalies from underlying rotation. Combined detection significance reaches $4.2$--$5.9\sigma$ by 2034 (depending on cross-correlation~$\rho$), offering robust tests this decade.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The nature of dark energy remains one of the most significant challenges in modern cosmology. While the $\Lambda$CDM model successfully describes many observations, it faces theoretical difficulties including the cosmological constant problem and coincidence problem, alongside growing observational tensions. Recent measurements reveal a $4.4\sigma$ discrepancy in the Hubble constant between early-universe (Planck: $H_0 = 67.36 \pm 0.54\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$)~\cite{Planck2018params} and late-universe (SH0ES: $H_0 = 73.04 \pm 1.04\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$)~\cite{Riess2022} determinations, alongside a $2.5\sigma$ tension in $\sigma_8$ between CMB and weak lensing measurements~\cite{KiDS2021}.

This work presents a theoretical framework that addresses these challenges through quantum gravitational effects in spin-torsion cosmology. Our approach builds on three theoretical pillars: (1) Loop Quantum Cosmology (LQC) providing a non-singular quantum bounce~\cite{Ashtekar2011}, (2) Einstein-Cartan theory incorporating fermionic spin-torsion coupling~\cite{Hehl1976}, and (3) false-vacuum bubble nucleation driving slow-roll inflation within a rotating black hole interior~\cite{Poplawski2016}.

\subsection{Theoretical Foundations and Novel Synthesis}

Our framework represents a novel synthesis of well-established theoretical components, never before combined into a unified cosmological model:

\textit{Einstein-Cartan Theory.}---The inclusion of spacetime torsion coupled to fermionic spin has been extensively studied since the 1970s. Popławski~\cite{Poplawski2012} demonstrated that torsion-induced four-fermion interactions can generate a small positive cosmological constant through condensate formation, providing a natural mechanism for dark energy generation. The Einstein-Cartan-Sciama-Kibble (ECSK) framework naturally avoids singularities through torsion-induced repulsion at extreme densities.

\textit{Loop Quantum Gravity and Parity Violation.}---The connection between LQG's Barbero-Immirzi parameter and parity-violating effects has been rigorously established by Freidel, Minic \& Takeuchi~\cite{Freidel2005} and Mercuri~\cite{Mercuri2006,Mercuri2009}. They showed that the Holst term in LQG, when coupled to fermions, generates effective four-fermion axial interactions that can violate parity.

\textit{Black Hole Universe Origin.}---Popławski~\cite{Poplawski2016} pioneered the ``universe in a black hole'' scenario, demonstrating that every black hole with torsion spawns a non-singular, closed baby universe beyond its event horizon. His subsequent work extended this to rotating black holes, showing that the baby universe inherits angular momentum and develops a preferred cosmic axis~\cite{Poplawski2019}.

\subsection{Original Contributions}

While building on these established foundations, our work makes several original contributions:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textit{Quantitative dark energy model}: First rigorous derivation of $\Leff = \Lconst + c_\omega\omega^2$ where $\Lconst = (\alpha/M)\Dinf$, with specific predictions $H_0 = 69.2 \pm 0.8\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$ and $\sigma_8 = 0.785 \pm 0.016$.

\item \textit{LQG--Einstein-Cartan integration}: First detailed synthesis combining LQG formalism (Barbero-Immirzi parameter, Holst action) with Einstein-Cartan cosmology.

\item \textit{Comprehensive observational framework}: Complete validation program with systematic error analysis, detection timelines, and falsification criteria.

\item \textit{Geometric dilution mechanism}: Novel derivation of how inflationary expansion naturally dilutes the primordial parity-odd coefficient to produce the observed dark energy scale.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Loop Quantum Cosmology and the Holst Action}

\subsubsection{Einstein-Cartan-Holst Action}

The fundamental action combining Einstein-Cartan theory with the Holst term is
\begin{align}\label{eq:ECH}
S_{\rm ECH} &= \frac{1}{16\pi G} \int d^4x\, e \left[R + \frac{1}{\gamma} \varepsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \right.\nonumber\\
&\qquad\left.+ \frac{1}{4} T^{abc} T_{abc}\right] + S_{\rm matter},
\end{align}
where $e$ is the tetrad determinant, $\gamma$ is the Barbero-Immirzi parameter, and $T^{abc}$ is the torsion tensor. The inclusion of the Holst term builds directly on the work of Freidel, Minic \& Takeuchi~\cite{Freidel2005}.

\subsubsection{Derivation of the Parity-Odd Term}

Starting with the complete action $S = S_{\rm gravity} + S_{\rm Holst} + S_{\rm fermion}$:

\textit{Step~1: Torsion Activation.}---When fermions are minimally coupled to gravity, torsion is activated through the spin density:
\begin{equation}
T^{abc} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\, S^{abc},
\end{equation}
where $S^{abc} = \frac{1}{4}\bar{\psi}\gamma^{[a}\gamma^{bc]}\psi$ is the fermionic spin density tensor.

\textit{Step~2: Four-Fermion Contact Interaction.}---Integrating out torsion yields an effective four-fermion interaction:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:4fermi}
\mathcal{L}_{\rm int} = -\frac{3\pi G_N}{2}\times \frac{\gamma^2}{\gamma^2+1}\times J_{(A)\mu}\,J^{\mu}_{(A)},
\end{equation}
where $J^{\mu}_{(A)}=\bar{\psi}\gamma^{\mu}\gamma^5\psi$ is the axial current.

\textit{Step~3: Parity-Odd Effective Action.}---The effective action acquires a parity-odd term~\cite{Mercuri2009}. Since $[K]=1$ and $[R]=2$ (mass units), the operator $\varepsilon^{abcd}K_{ab}R_{cd}$ has mass-dimension~3, so the coefficient carries mass-dimension $-1$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Seff}
S_{\rm eff} = \int d^4x\,\sqrt{-g}\;\frac{\alpha}{M}\;\varepsilon^{abcd}\,K_{ab}\,R_{cd},\quad [\alpha]=M^0.
\end{equation}

\textit{Step~4: One-Loop Coefficient.}---A regulator-transparent one-loop estimate in a torsionful background (with Nieh--Yan/Holst mixing~\cite{NiehYan1982}) yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:oneloop}
\frac{\alpha}{M}\sim \frac{g^2}{32\pi^2}\,\frac{\gamma}{M}\,\ln\!\left(\frac{\Lambda^2}{\mu^2}\right)+\delta_{\rm NY},
\end{equation}
where $g$ is the axial--torsion coupling, $\Lambda$ is a UV scale (\eg, an LQG area-gap mass), $\mu$ is the subtraction scale, and $\delta_{\rm NY}$ encodes finite renormalization of the Nieh--Yan density.

\subsubsection{Parameter Naturalness}

The parent black hole mass must satisfy $M_{\rm BH} > M_{\rm crit} = (M_{\rm Pl}^4/\rho_{\rm vac})^{1/3}\approx 10^{-3}M_\odot$, easily satisfied by any astrophysical black hole. For a parent with $M=10\,M_\odot$ and dimensionless spin $a_*=0.7$, the required dilution factor $\Omega_{\rm initial}/\Omega_{\rm BH}\approx 10^{-22}$ is naturally achieved through $\sim 50$ $e$-folds of inflation.

\subsection{Black Hole Interior and Quantum Bounce}

In Einstein-Cartan theory, the collapse is modified by torsion pressure. The modified Friedmann equation becomes
\begin{equation}\label{eq:modfriedmann}
H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\left[1-\frac{\rho}{\rhocrit}\right],
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhocrit}
\rhocrit = \frac{3}{8\pi G\,\gamma^2\,\Delta} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{32\pi^2\gamma^3}\,\rhoPl \simeq 0.41\,\rhoPl,
\end{equation}
with $\Delta = 4\sqrt{3}\pi\gamma\,\ell_P^2$ and $\rhoPl\equiv 1/G^2$ ($c=\hbar=1$). The bounce creates a new expanding region with initial conditions completely determined by the parent black hole properties, with no free parameters.

\subsection{Cosmic Rotation and Dark Energy}

\subsubsection{Covariant Formalism}

Using the $1+3$ covariant decomposition for an almost-FLRW congruence with shear $\sigma_{\mu\nu}$ and vorticity $\omega_{\mu\nu}$ (where $\omega^2\equiv\frac{1}{2}\omega_{\mu\nu}\omega^{\mu\nu}$), the generalized Friedmann constraint reads
\begin{equation}
H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho + \frac{\Lambda}{3}+\frac{1}{3}(\sigma^2-\omega^2)-\frac{k}{a^2}.
\end{equation}
Absorbing the shear and vorticity into an effective cosmological constant:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Leff}
\boxed{\Leff = \Lambda + (\sigma^2-\omega^2).}
\end{equation}
In the limit of negligible shear ($\sigma^2\ll\omega^2$), this reduces to $\Leff = \Lambda + c_\omega\omega^2$ with $c_\omega=-1$ in our conventions.

\textit{Crucial constraint.}---CMB isotropy bounds from Planck give $(\omega/H)_0 < 5\times 10^{-11}$ (95\% CL)~\cite{Saadeh2016}, implying $|c_\omega|\omega^2/H_0^2 < 10^{-20}$. Rotation is completely negligible for background expansion. Instead, rotation manifests only through parity-odd observables (CMB $E$-$B$ correlations, galaxy spin asymmetries) that probe the \textit{direction} of $\omega^a$, not its magnitude squared.

The observed dark energy scale arises from
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Lobs}
\Lambda_{\rm obs} = \left(\frac{\alpha}{M}\right)\Dinf \approx (2.3\;\text{meV})^4,
\end{equation}
where $\Dinf$ is the inflationary dilution factor (see Appendix~\ref{app:niehyan}).

\subsubsection{Galaxy Spin Alignment Mechanism}

The predicted galaxy spin asymmetry follows a dipole pattern $A_{\rm dipole}(\hat{n}) = A(z)\cos\theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle from the cosmic rotation axis. The amplitude evolves as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Az}
\boxed{A(z) = A_0\cdot(1+z)^{-p}\cdot e^{-qz},}
\end{equation}
with $A_0\in[0,0.02]$, $p\in[0,2]$, $q\in[0,2]$. The theoretical expectation is $A_0\sim 0.003$ based on the initial cosmic rotation.
