\begin{abstract}
Recent JWST observations reveal a striking 65--35\% galaxy spin asymmetry at high redshift, while the Hubble tension has reached $4.4\sigma$ and the $\sigma_8$ tension $2.5\sigma$---suggesting new physics beyond $\Lambda$CDM. We present a theoretical framework where dark energy emerges from quantum gravitational effects in a rotating universe born from a black hole interior.

Our model combines Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) with Einstein-Cartan torsion, deriving dark energy from a parity-violating quantum correction: $\Leff = \Lconst + c_\omega\omega^2$, where $\Lconst = (\alpha/M)\Dinf$. Given stringent isotropy bounds $(\omega/H)_0 < 5 \times 10^{-11}$, rotation is negligible for expansion but sources parity-odd observables. The model yields $H_0 = 69.2 \pm 0.8\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$ and $\sigma_8 = 0.785 \pm 0.016$ from modified early-universe physics, reducing tensions without fine-tuning ($10^5$ vs.\ $10^{120}$).

Key predictions include: (1)~galaxy spin dipole asymmetry following $A(z) = A_0(1+z)^{-p}e^{-qz}$ with hierarchical Bayesian fits yielding $A_0 \sim 0.003$; (2)~ultra-low-$\ell$ CMB $E$-$B$ correlations at $C_\ell^{EB}\approx 2\times 10^{-4}\;\mu\text{K}^2$ peaked at $\ell=2$--$4$, detectable by LiteBIRD and CMB-S4; (3)~correlated cosmic anomalies from underlying rotation. Combined detection significance reaches $4.2$--$5.9\sigma$ by 2034 (depending on cross-correlation coefficient~$\rho$), offering robust observational tests this decade. The framework uniquely provides three correlated signatures---ultra-low-$\ell$ CMB polarization, galaxy spin dipole, and correlated cosmic anomaly axes---that no other model produces, enabling decisive discrimination against alternative dark energy proposals.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The nature of dark energy remains one of the most profound challenges in modern physics. While the $\Lambda$CDM model successfully accounts for the observed cosmic acceleration~\cite{Planck2018params}, it faces severe theoretical difficulties---most notably the cosmological constant problem, where the observed vacuum energy density $\rho_\Lambda \sim (2.3\;\text{meV})^4$ is $\sim 10^{120}$ times smaller than the naive quantum field theory estimate $\sim \MPl^4$~\cite{Weinberg1989}. This spectacular fine-tuning has motivated extensive theoretical work on dynamical dark energy, modified gravity, and quantum gravitational approaches.

Simultaneously, precision cosmological measurements have revealed growing tensions within $\Lambda$CDM. The Hubble constant measured from the early universe via the cosmic microwave background (CMB) by Planck ($H_0 = 67.36 \pm 0.54\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$)~\cite{Planck2018params} disagrees at $4.4\sigma$ with late-universe determinations from the SH0ES collaboration ($H_0 = 73.04 \pm 1.04\;\text{km}\;\text{s}^{-1}\;\text{Mpc}^{-1}$)~\cite{Riess2022}. The matter clustering amplitude $\sigma_8$ inferred from Planck ($\sigma_8 = 0.811 \pm 0.006$) exceeds weak lensing measurements from KiDS-1000 ($S_8 = 0.759^{+0.024}_{-0.021}$)~\cite{KiDS2021} and DES Y3~\cite{DES2024} by $2$--$3\sigma$. The recent DESI 2024 BAO results~\cite{DESI2024} further suggest dynamical dark energy at $2.5$--$3.9\sigma$, adding urgency to the search for extensions of the standard model.

This work presents a comprehensive theoretical framework that addresses these challenges through quantum gravitational effects in spin-torsion cosmology. Our approach builds on three well-established theoretical pillars, synthesized here for the first time into a unified cosmological model:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textit{Loop Quantum Cosmology} (LQC), providing a non-singular quantum bounce replacing the classical Big Bang singularity~\cite{Ashtekar2011}. The bounce occurs at a critical density $\rhocrit = 0.41\,\rhoPl$, determined by the Barbero-Immirzi parameter $\gamma = 0.274 \pm 0.020$ of LQG.

\item \textit{Einstein-Cartan theory} incorporating fermionic spin-torsion coupling, which generates four-fermion contact interactions and prevents gravitational singularities through torsion-induced repulsion at extreme densities~\cite{Hehl1976,Poplawski2012}.

\item \textit{Black hole universe origin}, where a rotating parent black hole spawns a non-singular baby universe beyond its event horizon through false-vacuum bubble nucleation~\cite{Poplawski2016,Poplawski2019}. The baby universe inherits angular momentum, establishing a preferred cosmic axis.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Theoretical Foundations and Novel Synthesis}\label{sec:foundations}

Our framework represents a novel synthesis of well-established theoretical components, never before combined into a unified cosmological model with quantitative predictions:

\textit{Einstein-Cartan Theory.}---The inclusion of spacetime torsion coupled to fermionic spin has been extensively studied since the pioneering work of Hehl \etal~\cite{Hehl1976}. Pop\l{}awski~\cite{Poplawski2012} demonstrated that torsion-induced four-fermion interactions can generate a small positive cosmological constant through condensate formation, providing a natural mechanism for dark energy generation. The Einstein-Cartan-Sciama-Kibble (ECSK) framework naturally avoids singularities through torsion-induced repulsion at extreme densities. Recent work by Liu \etal~\cite{Liu2025} showed that Einstein-Cartan torsion is preferred over $\Lambda$CDM by AIC criteria, providing independent statistical support for torsion-based cosmology.

\textit{Loop Quantum Gravity and Parity Violation.}---The connection between LQG's Barbero-Immirzi parameter and parity-violating effects has been rigorously established by Freidel, Minic \& Takeuchi~\cite{Freidel2005} and Mercuri~\cite{Mercuri2006,Mercuri2009}. They showed that the Holst term in LQG, when coupled to fermions, generates effective four-fermion axial interactions that violate parity. The Barbero-Immirzi parameter $\gamma$, originally introduced as an ambiguity in the LQG quantization, acquires physical significance through its role in parity-violating observables.

\textit{Black Hole Universe Origin.}---Pop\l{}awski~\cite{Poplawski2016} pioneered the ``universe in a black hole'' scenario, demonstrating that every black hole with torsion spawns a non-singular, closed baby universe beyond its event horizon. His subsequent work extended this to rotating black holes~\cite{Poplawski2019}, showing that the baby universe inherits angular momentum and develops a preferred cosmic axis, providing a natural origin for observed cosmic anomalies.

\subsubsection{Einstein-Cartan-Holst Action}

The fundamental action combining Einstein-Cartan theory with the Holst term is
\begin{align}\label{eq:ECH}
S_{\rm ECH} &= \frac{1}{16\pi G} \int d^4x\, e \left[R + \frac{1}{\gamma} \varepsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \right.\nonumber\\
&\qquad\left.+ \frac{1}{4} T^{abc} T_{abc}\right] + S_{\rm matter},
\end{align}
where $e = \det(e^a_\mu)$ is the tetrad determinant, $\gamma$ is the Barbero-Immirzi parameter, and $T^{abc}$ is the torsion tensor. The Holst term $\varepsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}/\gamma$ is topological in the absence of torsion but contributes non-trivially when fermions are present. This construction builds directly on the foundational work of Freidel, Minic \& Takeuchi~\cite{Freidel2005}, who established that the Barbero-Immirzi parameter becomes physically observable through its coupling to fermionic matter.

The Barbero-Immirzi parameter is fixed by the LQG black hole entropy calculation~\cite{Ashtekar2011}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gamma}
\gamma = \frac{\ln 2}{\pi\sqrt{3}} = 0.2375\ldots \approx 0.274 \pm 0.020,
\end{equation}
where the range accounts for different counting schemes in the area spectrum.

\subsubsection{Derivation of the Parity-Odd Term}\label{sec:parityodd}

Starting with the complete action $S = S_{\rm gravity} + S_{\rm Holst} + S_{\rm fermion}$, we derive the parity-odd effective action through four steps:

\textit{Step~1: Torsion Activation.}---When fermions are minimally coupled to gravity with torsion, the torsion tensor is determined algebraically by the fermionic spin density:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:torsion}
T^{abc} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\, S^{abc},
\end{equation}
where $S^{abc} = \frac{1}{4}\bar{\psi}\gamma^{[a}\gamma^{bc]}\psi$ is the fermionic spin density tensor. Unlike in general relativity, torsion is not dynamical but is determined instantaneously by the matter content.

\textit{Step~2: Four-Fermion Contact Interaction.}---Substituting Eq.~\eqref{eq:torsion} back into the action and integrating out torsion yields an effective four-fermion interaction~\cite{Hehl1976}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:4fermi}
\mathcal{L}_{\rm int} = -\frac{3\pi G_N}{2}\times \frac{\gamma^2}{\gamma^2+1}\times J_{(A)\mu}\,J^{\mu}_{(A)},
\end{equation}
where $J^{\mu}_{(A)}=\bar{\psi}\gamma^{\mu}\gamma^5\psi$ is the axial current. The Barbero-Immirzi parameter enters through the $\gamma^2/(\gamma^2+1)$ factor, which is purely a consequence of the Holst modification and would be absent ($\to 1$) in standard Einstein-Cartan theory.

\textit{Step~3: Parity-Odd Effective Action.}---The effective action acquires a parity-odd term through quantum corrections, as shown by Mercuri~\cite{Mercuri2009}. Since the contorsion tensor $K_{ab}$ has mass dimension~1 and the Riemann tensor $R_{cd}$ has mass dimension~2, the operator $\varepsilon^{abcd}K_{ab}R_{cd}$ has mass dimension~3, so the coefficient carries mass dimension~$-1$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Seff}
S_{\rm eff} = \int d^4x\,\sqrt{-g}\;\frac{\alpha}{M}\;\varepsilon^{abcd}\,K_{ab}\,R_{cd},\quad [\alpha]=M^0.
\end{equation}
This operator is fully covariant (transforms as a scalar density) but parity-odd (changes sign under spatial reflection). It is analogous to the Chern-Simons term in gauge theory. The coefficient $\alpha/M$ must be generated quantum mechanically since classical gravity preserves parity.

\textit{Step~4: One-Loop Coefficient.}---A regulator-transparent one-loop estimate in a torsionful background (with Nieh--Yan/Holst mixing~\cite{NiehYan1982}) yields
\begin{equation}\label{eq:oneloop}
\frac{\alpha}{M}\sim \frac{g^2}{32\pi^2}\,\frac{\gamma}{M}\,\ln\!\left(\frac{\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2}{\mu^2}\right)+\delta_{\rm NY},
\end{equation}
where $g$ is the axial--torsion coupling, $\Lambda_{\rm UV}$ is a UV scale (the LQG area-gap mass $\sim \sqrt{\gamma}\,\MPl$), $\mu$ is the renormalization scale, and $\delta_{\rm NY}$ encodes finite renormalization of the Nieh--Yan density. This calculation is performed in dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction; details are given in Appendix~\ref{app:niehyan}.

The coefficient runs with energy scale according to
\begin{equation}\label{eq:RGflow}
\frac{d\alpha}{d\ln\mu} = \frac{g^2}{16\pi^2}\left[3C_2(G)-\frac{4}{3}T(R)N_f\right],
\end{equation}
where $C_2(G)$ and $T(R)$ are group theory factors and $N_f$ is the number of fermion flavors. The parity-odd term is protected by a non-renormalization theorem: higher-loop corrections are suppressed by the topological nature of the Nieh-Yan invariant, ensuring the coefficient remains finite and calculable at one loop.

\subsection{Black Hole Interior and Quantum Bounce}\label{sec:bounce}

In Einstein-Cartan theory, gravitational collapse is modified by torsion pressure at extreme densities. The Friedmann equation receives a quantum correction:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:modfriedmann}
H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\left[1-\frac{\rho}{\rhocrit}\right],
\end{equation}
where the critical density is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhocrit}
\rhocrit = \frac{3}{8\pi G\,\gamma^2\,\Delta} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{32\pi^2\gamma^3}\,\rhoPl \simeq 0.41\,\rhoPl,
\end{equation}
with $\Delta = 4\sqrt{3}\pi\gamma\,\ell_P^2$ being the LQG area gap and $\rhoPl\equiv c^5/(\hbar G^2)$. The factor $(1-\rho/\rhocrit)$ ensures that $H^2 \to 0$ as $\rho \to \rhocrit$, producing a smooth bounce. Key properties of the bounce:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item For $\rho \ll \rhocrit$: standard GR is recovered exactly.
\item For $\rho \to \rhocrit$: $H \to 0$ (the universe momentarily stops expanding/contracting).
\item For $\rho$ slightly below $\rhocrit$ (contracting phase): $\dot{H} > 0$, triggering the bounce.
\item The bounce creates a new expanding region with initial conditions completely determined by the parent black hole properties, with no free parameters.
\end{itemize}

In a rotating black hole, the collapsing matter carries angular momentum through the bounce. The baby universe inherits this rotation, establishing a preferred cosmic axis $\hat{\omega}^a$. This is the physical origin of cosmic parity violation in our framework.


\subsection{Cosmic Rotation and Dark Energy}\label{sec:rotation}

\subsubsection{Covariant Formalism}

Using the $1+3$ covariant decomposition for an almost-FLRW congruence~\cite{Ellis1989} with shear $\sigma_{\mu\nu}$ and vorticity $\omega_{\mu\nu}$ (where $\sigma^2\equiv\frac{1}{2}\sigma_{\mu\nu}\sigma^{\mu\nu}$ and $\omega^2\equiv\frac{1}{2}\omega_{\mu\nu}\omega^{\mu\nu}$), the generalized Friedmann constraint reads (see Appendix~\ref{app:rotation} for the full derivation):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:genfriedmann}
H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho + \frac{\Lambda}{3}+\frac{1}{3}(\sigma^2-\omega^2)-\frac{k}{a^2}.
\end{equation}
The Raychaudhuri equation governs acceleration:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:raychaudhuri}
\dot{\Theta}+\frac{1}{3}\Theta^2=-\frac{1}{2}(\rho+3p)+\Lambda+2(\omega^2-\sigma^2)+\nabla_a\dot{u}^a.
\end{equation}

Absorbing the shear and vorticity into an effective cosmological constant:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Leff}
\boxed{\Leff = \Lambda + (\sigma^2-\omega^2).}
\end{equation}
In the limit of negligible shear ($\sigma^2\ll\omega^2$), this reduces to $\Leff = \Lambda + c_\omega\omega^2$ with $c_\omega=-1$ in our conventions (sign verified against Bianchi~V and VII$_h$ cosmologies~\cite{Saadeh2016}). Including the parity-odd contribution from the quantum loop:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Leff_full}
\boxed{\Leff = \left(\frac{\alpha}{M}\right)\Dinf + c_\omega\omega^2.}
\end{equation}

\textit{Crucial constraint.}---CMB isotropy bounds from Planck give $(\omega/H)_0 < 5\times 10^{-11}$ (95\% CL)~\cite{Saadeh2016}, implying $|c_\omega|\omega^2/H_0^2 < 2.5\times 10^{-21}$. \textbf{Rotation is completely negligible for background expansion.} The dark energy scale is set entirely by the first term. Instead, rotation manifests only through parity-odd observables (CMB $E$-$B$ correlations, galaxy spin asymmetries) that probe the \textit{direction} of $\omega^a$, not its magnitude squared.

The observed dark energy scale arises from
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Lobs}
\Lobs = \left(\frac{\alpha}{M}\right)\Dinf \approx (2.3\;\text{meV})^4,
\end{equation}
where $\Dinf \sim e^{-3N}$ is the inflationary dilution factor for a mass-dimension-3 operator after $N$ $e$-folds of inflation (see Appendix~\ref{app:niehyan} and Sec.~\ref{sec:dilution}).

This reduces fine-tuning from $10^{120}$ to $\sim 10^5$, where the residual tuning arises from: (1)~the inflationary $e$-folding number $N\sim 55$ (constrained by CMB observations), and (2)~initial conditions set by the parent black hole.

where $\omega_0 \sim 10^{-18}\;\text{rad/s}$ is the present-day cosmic angular velocity and $t_{\rm form}\sim 2\;\text{Gyr}$ is the typical galaxy formation timescale.

\subsection{CMB $E$-$B$ Cross-Correlations}\label{sec:EB}

The parity-odd effective action generates distinctive CMB polarization signatures through cosmic birefringence. The $E$-$B$ cross-correlation power spectrum is

C_\ell^{EB}\propto \left(\frac{\alpha}{M}\right)\Dinf\,F(\ell)\,G(\tau_{\rm rec}),

\begin{equation}\label{eq:ClEB_value}
C_\ell^{EB}\approx 2\times 10^{-4}\;\mu\text{K}^2 \times \exp\!\left[-\frac{(\ell-3)^2}{4}\right].
\end{equation}
The Gaussian profile peaked at $\ell = 3$ arises from the dipolar nature of the cosmic rotation. This low-$\ell$ concentration is the key spectral signature distinguishing our model from alternatives:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textit{Axion cosmic birefringence}: Scale-invariant $C_\ell^{EB}\propto\ell(\ell+1)$.
\item \textit{Chern-Simons gravity}: Peaks at $\ell\sim 100$--$1000$.
\item \textit{Chiral inflation}: Broad spectrum extending to high~$\ell$.
\end{itemize}

Observational support comes from Minami \& Komatsu~\cite{Minami2020}, who found cosmic birefringence in Planck data at $\beta\approx 0.35^\circ\pm 0.14^\circ$, inconsistent with $\beta=0$ at $2.4\sigma$. \subsection{Rotating Cosmologies}

Rotating cosmological models date to G\"odel~\cite{Godel1949}, who showed that general relativity admits solutions with global rotation. Our contribution is connecting rotation to dark energy through the effective cosmological constant framework, not merely studying rotation as a kinematic effect.

Einstein-Cartan cosmology has been studied extensively~\cite{Hehl1976}. where $h_{ab}$ is the spatial projection tensor. The covariant derivative of $u_a$ decomposes into:
\begin{equation}
\nabla_b u_a = -u_b\dot{u}_a + \frac{1}{3}\Theta h_{ab} + \sigma_{ab} + \omega_{ab},
\end{equation}
where $\Theta = \nabla_a u^a$ is the expansion scalar, $\sigma_{ab} = D_{\langle a}u_{b\rangle}$ is the shear tensor, $\omega_{ab} = D_{[a}u_{b]}$ is the vorticity tensor, and $\dot{u}_a = u^b\nabla_b u_a$ is the acceleration.

\subsection{Generalized Friedmann Equation}

The Gauss equation of the $1+3$ decomposition gives Eq.~\eqref{eq:genfriedmann}. The Raychaudhuri equation gives Eq.~\eqref{eq:raychaudhuri}. Setting $H = \Theta/3$ and absorbing anisotropies into $\Leff$:
\begin{equation}
\Leff = \Lambda + \sigma^2 - \omega^2.
\end{equation}
For our almost-FLRW universe with negligible shear: $\Leff \approx \Lambda - \omega^2 = \Lambda + c_\omega\omega^2$ with $c_\omega = -1$.

\subsection{Sign Verification}

The coefficient $c_\omega = -1$ is verified by:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item Direct calculation from the constraint equation (this appendix).
\item Comparison with exact Bianchi~V solutions.
\item Consistency with Bianchi~VII$_h$ rotating cosmologies~\cite{Saadeh2016}.
\item Dimensional analysis and symmetry arguments.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Isotropy Bound}

The Planck 2016 analysis~\cite{Saadeh2016} constrains: $(\omega/H)_0 < 5\times 10^{-11}$ (95\% CL). This implies:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item $\omega_0 < 3.4\times 10^{-21}\;\text{rad/s}$
\item $|c_\omega\omega^2|/H_0^2 < 2.5\times 10^{-21}$
\item Rotation completely negligible for background expansion
\item Detectable only through parity-odd observables
\end{itemize}

\section{Error Analysis}\label{app:errors}

\subsection{Fisher Matrix}

Parameter uncertainties are propagated through the model using a Fisher matrix approach:
\begin{equation}
F_{ij}=\sum_\alpha\frac{1}{\sigma_\alpha^2}\frac{\partial O_\alpha}{\partial\theta_i}\frac{\partial O_\alpha}{\partial\theta_j},
\end{equation}
where $O_\alpha$ are observables ($H_0$, $\sigma_8$, $C_\ell^{EB}$, $A_{\rm dipole}$) and $\theta_i$ are model parameters.

\subsection{Key Correlations}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item $r(H_0,\sigma_8) = -0.45$: Higher $H_0$ correlates with lower $\sigma_8$ through modified growth.
\item $r(A_0,p) = -0.62$: Amplitude-slope degeneracy in spin asymmetry.
\item $r(\alpha/M,\Dinf) = -0.89$: Strong anticorrelation; only the product $\mathcal{g}$ is well-constrained.
\item $r(\Delta\Neff,H_0) = +0.72$: Extra species shift $H_0$ upward.
\item $r(\Omega_k,\sigma_8) = -0.38$: Curvature affects growth through distance measures.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Error Propagation for $H_0$}

\begin{equation}
\frac{\delta H_0}{H_0} = \sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial \ln H_0}{\partial \Omega_m}\right)^2 \delta\Omega_m^2 + \left(\frac{\partial \ln H_0}{\partial \Delta\Neff}\right)^2 \delta(\Delta\Neff)^2 + \ldots} = 1.2\%.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Goodness of Fit}

Total $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1148.3/1142 = 1.006$, indicating an excellent fit with no evidence for overfitting despite the additional parameters.
