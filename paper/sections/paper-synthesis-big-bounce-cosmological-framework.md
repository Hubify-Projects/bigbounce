### 1. Introduction

The standard Big Bang cosmological model, while remarkably successful in explaining the observed expansion of the universe, cosmic microwave background radiation, and primordial nucleosynthesis, contains a fundamental conceptual difficulty: the initial singularity. At t = 0, the classical Friedmann equations predict infinite density and curvature, signaling the breakdown of general relativity.

The Big Bounce paradigm offers a resolution by proposing that the universe underwent a prior contracting phase, reaching a maximum finite density before "bouncing" into the current expanding phase. This scenario is naturally realized in several theoretical frameworks:

1. **Loop Quantum Cosmology** — quantum geometry effects create an effective repulsive force at Planck density
2. **Einstein-Cartan Theory** — intrinsic spin of fermions generates torsion, which acts as a repulsive gravitational interaction at extreme densities
3. **Ekpyrotic/Cyclic Models** — brane collisions in string theory can generate bouncing cosmologies
