Figure X: Physical viability of the preferred ΔNeff region.

(a) Preliminary posterior distributions for ΔNeff from Paper 1 MCMC chains
(cohort_main, chains 2-7) under three dataset combinations: Planck+BAO (blue),
Planck+BAO+SN (red), and the full tension dataset (green). The yellow shaded
band indicates the preferred region ΔNeff ≈ 0.1-0.2 where the spin-torsion
model provides the best fit to data. The dashed vertical line marks the ΛCDM
value ΔNeff = 0.

(b) Observational upper bounds on ΔNeff from BBN (Yeh et al. 2022;
ΔNeff < 0.5), CMB (Planck 2018; ΔNeff < 0.34), ACT DR6 (Calabrese et al.
2025), and the Paper 1 preferred region. All constraints are mutually
compatible in the range ΔNeff ≈ 0.1-0.2.

(c) Reheating mechanism parameter space from the WP4 microphysics scan at
T_rh = 10^6 GeV, showing predicted ΔNeff as a function of the hidden-sector
degrees of freedom g_{*,hidden} and the dark radiation branching ratio Br_dr.
White dashed contours mark ΔNeff = 0.10 and 0.20; the yellow solid contour
marks ΔNeff = 0.15 (best fit). The darkened region is excluded by BBN and CMB
constraints. The orange contour highlights the overlap region where both
observational constraints are satisfied and the predicted ΔNeff falls within
the cosmologically preferred range. This demonstrates that a modest
hidden-sector particle content (g_{*,hidden} ~ 5-10) with small branching
ratios (Br_dr ~ 10^{-2}) naturally produces the preferred ΔNeff ~ 0.15.
