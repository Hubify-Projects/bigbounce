Mission Summary

This research mission refines Houston Golden's Big Bounce cosmological framework for arXiv submission. Houston has been developing these theoretical ideas about cosmology and white holes for years, originally inspired by studies at Cambridge. The paper presents a framework connecting quantum gravity corrections to observable cosmological signatures.

AI research assistants serve as autonomous collaborators that help with mathematical validation, literature review, data analysis, and quality assurance. The core ideas, theories, and creative insights are Houston's own — agents formalize and strengthen what Houston has been working on for years.

Research Question: Can a Big Bounce cosmological model with quantum gravity corrections produce testable predictions consistent with current observational data, and is the paper presenting this model rigorous enough for arXiv submission?

Methodology Overview

Phase 1 — Literature Review: Atlas surveys the cosmology field, maps prior work on bounce models and quantum gravity, identifies gaps where Houston's framework makes novel contributions.

Phase 2 — Mathematical Validation: Tensor verifies all equations, runs numerical checks on predictions, ensures dimensional consistency and mathematical rigor.

Phase 3 — Data Analysis: Nova explores alternative interpretations of observational data; Tensor compares model predictions to CMB data, BAO measurements, and other cosmological observations.

Phase 4 — Paper Synthesis: Sage integrates all findings into a coherent manuscript with proper citations, clear argumentation, and transparent acknowledgment of AI assistance.

Phase 5 — Peer Review: Keane conducts rigorous simulated peer review, identifying areas for improvement and ensuring submission readiness for arXiv.
