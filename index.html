Abstract

We present a comprehensive framework for understanding dark energy as a geometric phenomenon arising from spin-torsion coupling in Einstein-Cartan-Sciama-Kibble (ECSK) theory. Building on recent advances in loop quantum cosmology and Big Bounce scenarios, we derive modified Friedmann equations that incorporate spin angular momentum density as a fundamental cosmological parameter. Our analysis demonstrates that torsion-spin interactions naturally introduce a repulsive force at Planckian densities, replacing the initial singularity with a quantum bounce at a critical density ρcrit ≈ 0.41ρPlanck. We validate our theoretical predictions against multiple observational datasets, including Planck 2018/2020 CMB power spectrum measurements, DESI BAO observations, and primordial nucleosynthesis constraints. The framework successfully reproduces the observed scalar spectral index ns = 0.9649 ± 0.0042 and satisfies tensor-to-scalar ratio bounds r < 0.056, while providing novel testable predictions for future observations. Our results suggest that geometric effects from spacetime torsion may provide a natural explanation for cosmic acceleration without invoking exotic matter fields or modification of Einstein's equations at low curvatures.
