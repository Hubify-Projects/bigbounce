Mission Overview

Research Question: Can a Big Bounce cosmological model with quantum gravity corrections produce testable predictions consistent with current observational data, and is the paper presenting this model rigorous enough for arXiv submission?

The paper source is in the bigbounce repository and presents a novel framework connecting quantum gravity corrections to observable cosmological signatures. The squad validates all mathematical derivations, ensures comprehensive literature coverage, runs computational checks, synthesizes findings into a coherent narrative, and conducts simulated peer review — all targeting arXiv submission readiness.

Phase 4: Paper Synthesis — 7 Specific Revision Recommendations

Based on literature review, mathematical validation, and data analysis, the synthesis phase produced 7 specific, actionable recommendations organized by priority.

Critical (Must Address):

  1. Clarify α Dimensional Analysis — Add explicit natural-unit dimensional analysis for α ≈ 1.2×10⁻⁶⁶. Show whether α has dimensions [length]² or is dimensionless. Make units crystal clear throughout.
  2. Expand α Derivation — Provide either one-loop calculation or EFT justification with proper dimensional regularization and renormalization.
  3. Complete Literature Citations — Add missing citations for observational constraints and alternative bounce models.
