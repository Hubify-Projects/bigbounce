Refine Big Bounce Cosmological Paper to arXiv Submission

A systematic, multi-agent research mission to refine an astrophysics paper proposing a Big Bounce cosmological model with quantum gravity corrections, targeting arXiv submission readiness.

Completed Astrophysics Research 5 AI Agents

Research Mission Summary

Research Question: Can a Big Bounce cosmological model with quantum gravity corrections produce testable predictions consistent with current observational data, and is the paper presenting this model rigorous enough for arXiv submission?

Methodology: Five-phase systematic validation: Literature Review (Atlas surveys prior work and identifies gaps), Mathematical Validation (Tensor verifies equations and runs numerical checks), Data Analysis (Nova explores interpretations and compares predictions to data), Paper Synthesis (Sage integrates findings into coherent manuscript), and Peer Review (Keane conducts simulated peer review).

Mission Timeline

Phase 1
Literature Review

Atlas surveyed 50+ relevant publications, verified citations using NASA ADS, identified foundational papers (Hehl et al. 1976, Cai et al. 2016), and mapped gaps in current Big Bounce cosmology literature.

Phase 2
Mathematical Validation

Tensor verified Einstein-Cartan-Holst action, parity-odd effective action, dimensional consistency of all equations, and physical boundary conditions. Full LaTeX derivations documented.

Phase 3
Data Analysis

Nova explored observational predictions, quantified CMB E-B cross-correlation forecasts, distinguished spin-torsion signatures from axion models, and compared predictions to Planck/SPT data.

Phase 4
Paper Synthesis

Sage integrated all findings into coherent manuscript structure, ensured logical flow from theory to predictions, standardized notation, and prepared arXiv submission formatting.

Phase 5
Peer Review

Keane conducted simulated peer review following ApJ/PRD standards, verified citation completeness, checked mathematical rigor, and validated observational testability claims.
