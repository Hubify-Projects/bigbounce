Abstract

The Hubble tension (\({\sim}4.9\sigma\)), the \(\sigma_8\) tension (\(2.5\sigma\)), and growing evidence for cosmic parity violation—including \(2.4\)–\(2.7\sigma\) cosmic birefringence from Planck, with independent confirmation at \(2.9\sigma\) from ACT DR6—suggest new physics beyond \(\Lambda\)CDM. This paper presents a framework where dark energy emerges from quantum gravitational effects in a rotating universe born from a black hole interior. The model combines Loop Quantum Gravity with Einstein-Cartan torsion, modeling dark energy as arising from a parity-violating quantum correction: \(\Lambda_{\rm eff} \approx \Xi\,M_{\rm Pl}^2\), where the inflationary suppression factor \(\Xi = [(\alpha/M)\,M_{\rm Pl}]\,\mathcal{D}_{\rm inf}\) is fixed by early-universe physics (the late-time \(w=-1\) behavior is assumed, not derived).

Core Equation

The effective cosmological constant receives contributions from a one-loop parity-odd quantum correction, diluted by inflation:

\[\boxed{\Lambda_{\rm eff} \approx \Xi\,M_{\rm Pl}^2}\]

\(\Xi \equiv [(\alpha/M)\,M_{\rm Pl}]\,\mathcal{D}_{\rm inf} \sim 10^{-123}\) — the inflationary suppression factor

The parity-odd coefficient \(\alpha/M\) is generated at one loop from the Einstein-Cartan-Holst action coupled to fermions. The inflationary dilution factor \(\mathcal{D}_{\rm inf} = e^{-3N_{\rm tot}}\times(T_{\rm reh}/M_{\rm GUT})^{3/2}\) converts the Planck-scale parity-odd effect into the observed dark energy scale \(\rho_\Lambda \approx (2.3\;\text{meV})^4\), translating the cosmological constant problem from “why is \(\Lambda\) so small?” to “why did inflation last \(\sim\)92 total \(e\)-folds?”—a question with potential dynamical answers.

Derivation Chain

The dark energy scale is derived through a continuous chain from Planck-scale microphysics to observational cosmology:

1. Einstein-Cartan-Holst Action

The Barbero-Immirzi parameter \(\gamma = 0.274 \pm 0.020\) from LQG black hole entropy counting enters the Holst term, coupling to fermions and generating parity-violating four-fermion interactions.

2. One-Loop Parity-Odd Coefficient

A fermion loop with axial-torsion vertex and graviton vertex generates the finite parity-odd operator \((\alpha/M)\,\varepsilon^{abcd}K_{ab}R_{cd}\) after Nieh-Yan counterterm subtraction. The dimensionless loop factor is \([(\alpha/M)\,M_{\rm Pl}] \sim 10^{-2}\).

3. Quantum Bounce

Torsion-regulated collapse in a rotating black hole interior produces a non-singular bounce at \(\rho_{\rm crit} \simeq 0.27\,\rho_{\rm Pl}\), spawning a baby universe that inherits angular momentum. No free parameters—all quantities fixed by \(\gamma\).

4. Inflationary Dilution

The fermion spin density dilutes as \(a^{-3}\) during \(N_{\rm tot} \approx 92\) total \(e\)-folds of inflation, suppressing the primordial parity-odd coefficient by \(\mathcal{D}_{\rm inf} \sim 10^{-121}\). The result: \(\rho_\Lambda = \Xi\,M_{\rm Pl}^4 \approx (2.3\;\text{meV})^4\).

5. Observational Signatures

Three distinctive, testable signatures: CMB \(E\)-\(B\) correlations from the parity-odd sector (birefringence angle not derived; requires photon-torsion coupling), galaxy spin dipole asymmetry \(A(z) \approx 0.003(1+z)^{-0.5}e^{-z/2}\) (empirical), and correlated cosmic anomaly axes.

Three Distinctive Signatures

The framework accommodates three correlated observational signatures providing a distinctive, testable set:

CMB \(E\)-\(B\) Correlations

Isotropic cosmic birefringence producing \(C_\ell^{EB} \approx 2\beta(C_\ell^{EE} - C_\ell^{BB})\) across all \(\ell\). The rotation angle \(\beta\) is not derived in this work (requires a photon-torsion coupling). The Planck measurement of \(\beta \approx 0.30°\) at \(2.4\)–\(2.7\sigma\) is qualitatively consistent with the framework's parity violation. Independent confirmation at \(2.9\sigma\) from ACT DR6 strengthens the case. Testable by LiteBIRD (2030) and CMB-S4.

Galaxy Spin Dipole

Dipole amplitude \(A(z) = A_0(1+z)^{-p}e^{-qz}\) with \(A_0 \sim 0.003\) and axis at \((l \sim 52^\circ, b \sim 68^\circ)\). Testable with LSST (\(10^9\) galaxies by 2030). Hierarchical Bayesian framework accounts for survey-specific systematics.

Correlated Anomaly Axes

CMB anomaly axis, galaxy spin dipole axis, and birefringence axis should all align with the cosmic rotation axis \(\hat{\omega}^a\). This triple correlation is a smoking-gun signature testable via cross-correlation analysis.

Experimental Outlook

The framework's signatures are testable with forthcoming experiments (LiteBIRD, CMB-S4, LSST). Forecasting specific detection timelines is premature without derived signal amplitudes. See the paper's falsification criteria for explicit conditions under which the framework would be ruled out.

Falsification Criteria

The model defines explicit conditions under which it would be ruled out:

  • No CMB \(E\)-\(B\) detection at \(>2\sigma\) by CMB-S4 + LiteBIRD combined (~2032)
  • \(E\)-\(B\) spectrum shape inconsistent with \(2\beta(C_\ell^{EE}-C_\ell^{BB})\) across all \(\ell\)
  • No galaxy spin dipole in LSST at \(>3\sigma\) with \(>10^9\) galaxies (~2030)
  • Dipole axis inconsistent with CMB anomaly axis by \(>30^\circ\)
  • \(H_0\) converges on 67.4 or 73.0 without intermediate value
  • \(\Delta N_{\rm eff}\) excluded from the ranges accommodated by the extended parameter space
