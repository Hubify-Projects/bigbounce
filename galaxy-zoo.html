Connection to the paper: The BigBounce framework predicts a galaxy spin dipole asymmetry following \(A(z) = A_0(1+z)^{-p}e^{-qz}\), with hierarchical Bayesian fits yielding \(A_0 \sim 0.003\). Galaxy Zoo DESI provides 8.67 million galaxies with spiral direction (Z-wise/S-wise) classifications — the largest dataset available for testing this signature.

Spin Asymmetry vs. Redshift

Galaxy spin asymmetry \(A(z)\) measured in 20 redshift bins, with the paper's phenomenological model overlay.

\[A(z) = A_0 \cdot (1+z)^{-p} \cdot e^{-qz}, \quad A_0 \approx 0.003,\; p \approx 0.5,\; q \approx 0.5\]
Figure 1. Galaxy spin asymmetry \(A(z) = (N_{\rm CW} - N_{\rm CCW})/(N_{\rm CW} + N_{\rm CCW})\) in redshift bins. Circles: data points with binomial errors. Dashed line: paper model \(A(z) = 0.003(1+z)^{-0.5}e^{-0.5z}\). The asymmetry is ~0.3% at low redshift and decays at higher z, consistent with the framework's phenomenological prediction.

Spiral Direction Distribution

Overall distribution of spiral arm winding direction across the Galaxy Zoo catalogs. A small but persistent excess of clockwise (Z-wise) spirals is observed.

Figure 2. Spiral direction breakdown from Galaxy Zoo 2 (304,122 galaxies). Z-wise (CW): 54,742 — S-wise (CCW): 51,700 — a ~5.6% relative excess, with the overall asymmetry diluted by uncertainties in edge-on and ambiguous classifications.

Volunteer Classification Agreement

Galaxy Zoo relies on citizen science — each galaxy is classified by ~44 volunteers on average. The agreement rate varies by morphological type.

Figure 3. Mean volunteer agreement rate by morphological category. Ellipticals and edge-on disks show high agreement (>85%), while spiral direction has lower agreement (~72%) due to inherent ambiguity — this noise is accounted for in the hierarchical Bayesian model.
