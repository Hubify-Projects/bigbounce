Human-AI Collaboration Model

Transparent Authorship Framework

Houston's Role (Principal Investigator)

  • Develops core theoretical insights and cosmological framework
  • Defines research questions and mission objectives
  • Provides creative direction and conceptual innovations
  • Makes final authorship and publication decisions
  • Ensures theoretical coherence with his broader Big Bounce vision

Primary Author: Houston Golden is the sole author of the paper. The ideas, theories, and creative insights are his.

AI Agents' Role (Research Assistants)

  • Formalize mathematical frameworks and derive equations
  • Validate theoretical consistency and physical plausibility
  • Conduct comprehensive literature reviews and citation management
  • Perform numerical analysis and computational verification
  • Ensure academic rigor and arXiv submission standards

Acknowledgment: AI assistance transparently disclosed in paper acknowledgments section.

Why This Model Works

Traditional academic research often separates creative insight from technical execution. Houston's human-AI collaboration model recognizes that theoretical innovation comes from human intuition and creativity, while formalization and validation benefit from AI's computational rigor and literature access.

This is not AI "writing a paper" — it's AI serving as a highly capable research assistant that helps Houston refine, validate, and communicate his ideas to academic standards. The analogy: a theoretical physicist (Houston) working with a team of postdocs (AI agents) who handle derivations, literature surveys, and mathematical checks while the PI provides the vision.

"The Big Bounce framework is mine. The agents help me prove it's right — or show me where I'm wrong so I can refine it. That's good science."

— Houston Golden, on the human-AI collaboration model
