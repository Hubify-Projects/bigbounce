1. Introduction

The classical Big Bang cosmology faces a fundamental singularity problem at the origin of the universe, where general relativity predicts infinite density and curvature. This breakdown of classical physics motivates the search for quantum gravity corrections that could resolve the initial singularity through a "Big Bounce" scenario, where the universe transitions from a contracting to an expanding phase at finite density.

Recent advances in Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG), string gas cosmology, and Einstein-Cartan theory with spin-torsion coupling have provided promising frameworks for bounce cosmology. However, the challenge remains to develop models that are both mathematically rigorous and observationally testable. The goal of this research is to refine a Big Bounce cosmological model incorporating quantum gravity corrections to produce concrete, falsifiable predictions that can be compared against current observational data from cosmic microwave background (CMB) measurements, baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO), and other cosmological probes.

This paper presents a comprehensive analysis of a torsion-corrected bounce model, validates its mathematical foundations, and evaluates its compatibility with current observational constraints. Our ultimate aim is to produce a manuscript meeting the standards for submission to the arXiv preprint server and subsequent peer-reviewed publication.

where \(H\) is the Hubble parameter, \(G\) is the gravitational constant, \(\rho\) is the energy density, \(k\) is a coupling constant characterizing the strength of torsion effects, and \(\sigma^2\) represents the spin density squared.

Bounce Condition

The critical density at which the bounce occurs is derived by setting \(H = 0\) and solving for the density at the turning point:

\[ \rho_c = \frac{3k\sigma^2}{8\pi G} \]

This establishes a finite maximum density determined by quantum gravity corrections, preventing the classical singularity. The bounce condition requires that the torsion term becomes dynamically significant at Planckian densities, providing a natural ultraviolet cutoff for gravitational collapse.

Equation of State Near Bounce

Analysis of the effective equation of state parameter \(w = p/\rho\) near the bounce reveals a transition consistent with \(w \to -1\) at the turning point. This behavior is required for the bounce to occur smoothly without violating energy conditions in the effective fluid description. The verification confirms that:

  • The equation of state evolves continuously through the bounce
  • No exotic matter with super-negative pressure is required
  • The transition respects causality and thermodynamic constraints

These mathematical validations establish the internal consistency of the torsion-corrected bounce model and provide confidence in its theoretical foundations.
