Our research employs a five-phase methodology: (1) comprehensive literature review mapping the current state of bounce cosmology, (2) rigorous mathematical validation of modified Friedmann equations with torsion corrections, (3) data analysis comparing theoretical predictions against observational constraints, (4) synthesis of findings into a coherent manuscript, and (5) simulated peer review to ensure publication readiness. The standard cosmological model, while extraordinarily successful in explaining the evolution of the universe from early times to the present, encounters a fundamental singularity at the Big Bang. This initial singularity represents a breakdown of classical general relativity, suggesting the necessity of quantum gravitational corrections at extreme energy densities. The Big Bounce scenario offers an alternative framework wherein quantum effects prevent the singularity, replacing it with a smooth transition from a contracting to an expanding phase.

3. Mathematical Framework

3.1 Modified Friedmann Equations with Torsion Corrections

Derivation and Verification

Source: astro-tensor-v1 (Mathematical Validation, Feb 15, 2026)

In Einstein-Cartan theory, the inclusion of spacetime torsion coupled to intrinsic spin leads to modifications of the standard Friedmann equation. The verified torsion-corrected Friedmann equation takes the form:

\[ H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - k\sigma^2 \]

where:

  • \(H\) is the Hubble parameter
  • \(G\) is Newton's gravitational constant
  • \(\rho\) is the energy density
  • \(k\) is a coupling constant characterizing the torsion-spin interaction
  • \(\sigma\) is the spin density magnitude

The crucial insight is that the torsion term \(-k\sigma^2\) provides a negative contribution that becomes dominant at high densities, preventing the singularity.

3.2 Bounce Condition

The bounce occurs when the Hubble parameter reaches zero, \(H = 0\), marking the transition from contraction to expansion. At this critical point:

\[ 0 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho_c - k\sigma^2 \]

Solving for the critical density at bounce:

\[ \rho_c = \frac{3k\sigma^2}{8\pi G} \]

This critical density represents the maximum energy density achieved by the universe, occurring at the bounce point. The bounce is thus a natural consequence of the quantum correction terms, requiring no fine-tuning or exotic matter.

3.3 Equation of State Near the Bounce

The equation of state parameter \(w = p/\rho\) (where \(p\) is pressure) characterizes the matter content of the universe. Near the bounce, the verified analysis shows consistency with a transition through \(w = -1\), corresponding to a brief phase of vacuum-like behavior:

\[ w \approx -1 + \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{\rho - \rho_c}{\rho_c}\right) \]

This behavior is essential for the smoothness of the bounce and has implications for primordial perturbations that propagate through the transition. The consistency of this transition has been mathematically verified and represents a key prediction of the model.
