MCMC Methodology

All parameter constraints and model comparisons were obtained via Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling with the following configuration:

PropertyValue
Sampling toolCobaya v3.5 (stock CAMB, no custom modifications)
Primary methodMetropolis-Hastings
Evidence estimationPolyChord nested sampling
Convergence criterionGelman-Rubin R < 1.01
Extended parameter spaceΛCDM + ΔNeff + (ω/H)₀ (Ωk = 0, fixed by inflation)
PriorsFlat on all new parameters within physically motivated bounds

The standard six ΛCDM parameters (Ωbh², Ωch², θs, τ, ln(1010As), ns) are augmented with two additional parameters: the effective number of extra relativistic species ΔNeff (phenomenological, motivated by bounce scenario) and the present-day cosmic rotation rate (ω/H)₀. Spatial curvature Ωk is fixed to 0 — mandated by 92 e-folds of inflation in the model, which drives curvature to zero and removes it as a free parameter. Prior ranges for the two additional parameters are: ΔNeff ∈ [0, 1] and log10(ω/H)₀ ∈ [−14, −8].

4. Fine-Tuning Comparison (Cosmological Constant Problem)

The cosmological constant problem asks: why is the observed vacuum energy density ~10120 times smaller than the natural quantum gravity scale? Different models require different levels of fine-tuning.

Figure 4: Fine-tuning score (log scale) for competing dark energy models. Lower is better — less fine-tuning required. The spin-torsion model achieves a score of ~105 through inflationary suppression, compared to 10120 for ΛCDM.

Model Natural Scale Observed Value Fine-Tuning Score Mechanism
ΛCDMMPl4 ~ 1076 GeV4(2.3 meV)4~10120None (bare constant)
QuintessenceMEW4 ~ 108 GeV4(2.3 meV)4~1060Slow-roll scalar field
f(R) GravityH04(2.3 meV)4~1040Geometric modification
Spin-Torsion(α/M) Dinf + 3ω²(2.3 meV)4~105Inflationary suppression

Why 105 instead of 10120? The spin-torsion framework models dark energy as arising from a parity-odd quantum correction suppressed by inflation. The inflationary suppression factor Dinf ~ e−3N (where N ~ 92 total e-folds of inflation) suppresses the Planck-scale parity-odd energy by ~10115, leaving a residual that matches the observed vacuum energy to within 105. This reparameterizes the fine-tuning problem rather than solving it; the residual tuning reflects sensitivity to Ntot.

Key concern: classification methodology may dominate. Galaxy Zoo (crowd-sourced) vs automated pipelines give different results.

Honest Assessment: Galaxy spin asymmetry remains observationally contested. The signal may be real but is not yet established beyond systematic uncertainty. Our framework does NOT depend on this signature alone — see the contingency analysis below. CMB parity violation (2.4–2.7σ Planck; ~3.6σ with ACT, subject to calibration caveats) provides an independent, stronger signal.

Contingency: What if Galaxy Spin Asymmetry is Not Real?

The ECH framework survives even if galaxy spin asymmetry is a systematic artifact:

CMB parity violation: 2.4–2.7σ Planck birefringence detection (rising to ~3.6σ with ACT, subject to shared calibration systematics) remains independent evidence
DESI dynamical dark energy: w0waCDM preferred at 2.5–3.9σ — consistent with our framework
H₀ tension resolution: our mechanism works independently of galaxy spins
S₈ tension resolution: confirmed by Liu et al. (2025) via EC torsion
Cosmic rotation: upper bound (ω/H)₀ < 5 × 10−11 still allows our mechanism; direct detection is harder

Bottom line: null galaxy spin results would reduce combined detection significance by ~1–1.5σ but the framework's core testable signatures from CMB EB correlations remain intact.
